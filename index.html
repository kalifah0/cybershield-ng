// CYBERSECURITY SAFETY TIPS
01
Never share your OTP. No legitimate bank, CBN official, EFCC agent, or police officer will ever ask for your One-Time Password. Hang up or walk away immediately if asked.
02
Verify before you transfer. Always call back the person requesting a transfer using a number you already know — not a number they provide. Fraudsters impersonate family members and colleagues.
03
Use strong, unique passwords. Do not use the same password across your banking apps. Enable biometric authentication where available. Change passwords every 3 months.
04
Check URLs carefully. Always confirm the website address starts with https:// and matches your bank's exact official domain before entering any login credentials.
05
Protect your BVN. Your Bank Verification Number is not a password — but scammers use it to impersonate you. Never share it publicly, online, or over the phone.
06
Beware of SIM swap fraud. If your phone suddenly loses signal, contact your network provider immediately. Fraudsters use SIM swaps to bypass two-factor authentication.
07
Report immediately. If you suspect fraud, call your bank's official helpline right away. Also report to: EFCC — 08093322644 | CBN — 07002255226 | NITDA — report.nitda.gov.ng
08
Update your apps regularly. App updates often contain critical security patches. Enable automatic updates for all banking and financial applications on your phone.

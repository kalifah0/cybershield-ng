Cybersecurity Awareness
Essential safety tips for every Nigerian navigating digital financial services
01
Never Share Your OTP
No bank, CBN official, EFCC agent or police officer will ever ask for your One-Time Password. Hang up immediately if asked — it is always a scam.
02
Verify Before You Transfer
Always call back using a number you already know — never one they provide. Fraudsters routinely impersonate family members and work colleagues.
03
Use Strong Unique Passwords
Never reuse passwords across banking apps. Enable biometric login everywhere. Change all passwords every 90 days as a habit.
04
Check Website URLs Carefully
Confirm the URL begins with https:// and matches your bank's exact official domain before entering any credentials. Look for subtle misspellings.
05
Protect Your BVN
Your BVN is used by scammers to impersonate you at financial institutions. Never share it publicly, online or over the phone with anyone.
06
Beware SIM Swap Fraud
If your phone loses signal unexpectedly, call your network provider immediately. Fraudsters use SIM swaps to intercept your 2FA codes.
07
Report Fraud Immediately
EFCC: 08093322644 | CBN: 07002255226 | NITDA: report.nitda.gov.ng — report within 24 hours for the best chance of recovery.
08
Keep Your Apps Updated
App updates contain critical security patches that close known vulnerabilities. Enable automatic updates for all banking and payment applications on your phone.
